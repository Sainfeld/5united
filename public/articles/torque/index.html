Torque Specifications for the /5 motorcycles

Warning: The following page is not currently ready for use. Don't assume anything is accurate. You've been warned!

The main reason for a spec on tightness is to prevent over tightening, not under tightening. In the case of multiple fasteners on one part, it is also to prevent warping.

Carefully check for the torque on the part that you are tightening or loosening. One of the BMW shop manuals had incorrect torque settings in it. Some of the after market books have not always gotten it correct. Use common sense and double check it. [1]

The following page is not designed to be comprehensive, but a collection of torque specifications related to the more common fasteners. It is a collaborative effort of the 5united community.

Convert Newton-metre to Foot-Pound force

Nuts on the head studs

23-27 foot-pounds [3]

Cylinder studs have had a variety of torque settings published, some in error. The true story, as in a BMW bulletin, is that the torque for ALL models at these studs should be 25 +- 2 footpounds, and the target is 25, NOT 27, and definitely not 29-31, as in the old /5 manual days. If your torque wrench is quite accurate, you may be able to use 27 or 28.

Snowbum

Finned exhaust nuts

101-130 foot-pounds [2]

There is NO GOOD REASON to have these nuts so tight. I don't know how tight I put them, but I suspect it is not over 90 foot-pounds. I never do them with a torque wrench, I always do it by feel, a decent grunt, using a proper finned wrench, and NOT too darn tight.

Snowbum

Transmission drain plug

14-18.5 foot-pounds [2]

Transmission fill plug

20-22 foot-pounds [2]

Rocker arm adjustment lock nut

13-16 foot-pounds [2]

some say 12, and I agree

Oil pan mounting bolts

5.5-6 foot-pounds == 66-72 inch-pounds [2]

Careful not to overtighten. Its easy to strip the threads and spend the next several hours exploring how best to use helicoils and/or timesert repair kits

Eric

Engine oil pan drain

20-22 foot-pounds [2]

Book values have been variously from 20-25. I tend to use the low side of spec for this pan drain bolt.

Snowbum

The bottom caps of the front forks.

Isn’t the spec something like 80 ft-lbs? Why? If it holds in the fork oil, and the cap is tight enough to keep it from jiggling back and forth, why tighter? It’s can’t go anywhere.
